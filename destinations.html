Colombo

The Commercial Capital

A vibrant city where high-rise buildings meet colonial charm. World-class malls, rooftop restaurants, Galle Face promenade, and a buzzing nightlife make Colombo the island's dynamic gateway.

Negombo

The Fishing Town Gateway

A laid-back coastal town just minutes from the airport, famous for its colourful fishing boats, Dutch canals, seafood, and long sandy beaches ideal for a first or last-night stay.

Enquire Now

Bentota

A Charming Coastal Retreat

Where the Bentota River meets the Indian Ocean. A serene resort town with water sports, Ayurveda spas, turtle hatcheries, and the tranquil Brief Garden designed by Bevis Bawa.

Enquire Now

Galle

Colonial Fort City

A UNESCO World Heritage site where Dutch colonial bastions overlook the Indian Ocean. Cobblestone streets, boutique cafés, lighthouse sunsets, and centuries of layered history await.

Enquire Now

Hikkaduwa

Coral Reefs & Surf Breaks

Sri Lanka's surf capital on the south-west coast. Crystal-clear waters over living coral reefs, vibrant beach bars, glass-bottom boat rides, and a lively backpacker scene.

Enquire Now

Mirissa

Whale Watching Capital

Home to the world's greatest blue whale watching experience. A crescent-shaped bay with calm turquoise waters, coconut-fringed beaches, and spectacular seafood shacks at sunset.

Enquire Now

Tangalle

Unspoiled Southern Coast

One of Sri Lanka's most beautiful and unspoiled stretches of coastline. Leatherback turtle nesting beaches, quiet lagoons, and boutique eco-resorts set among tropical gardens.

Enquire Now

Yala

Wildlife Safari Paradise

Sri Lanka's most visited national park, boasting the world's highest density of wild leopards. Spot elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and hundreds of bird species on thrilling game drives.

Enquire Now

Arugam Bay

Sri Lanka's Surf Capital

Rated among the world's top ten surf spots, Arugam Bay draws wave-riders from across the globe. Laid-back beach shacks, elephant sightings at dusk, and a bohemian traveller vibe.

Enquire Now

Pasikuda

Crystal-Clear Shallow Waters

Famous for its exceptionally calm, shallow bay stretching nearly a kilometre out to sea. Powder-white sand, turquoise water, and luxury beach resorts make Pasikuda a true hidden gem.

Enquire Now

Trincomalee

Natural Harbour & Hot Springs

One of the world's finest natural deep-water harbours. Stunning Nilaveli beach, the sacred Koneswaram Hindu temple on a cliff top, and natural hot springs at Kanniyai nearby.

Enquire Now

Jaffna

Rich Tamil Culture & Heritage

Sri Lanka's cultural capital of the north — vibrant Hindu kovils, a magnificent Dutch colonial fort, palm-fringed islands, and a unique cuisine that differs entirely from the rest of the island.

Enquire Now

Anuradhapura

Ancient Buddhist Kingdom

A UNESCO World Heritage city and one of Asia's oldest civilisations. Colossal dagobas rising above the jungle, the sacred Sri Maha Bodhi tree grown from the Buddha's own fig tree, and ancient reservoirs.

Enquire Now

Kandy

Cultural Capital of Sri Lanka

The last royal capital of Sri Lanka, set around a beautiful lake surrounded by misty hills. The Temple of the Tooth Relic, the Royal Botanical Gardens, and vibrant Kandyan dance performances await.

Enquire Now

Sigiriya

The Lion Rock Fortress

One of the eight UNESCO World Heritage Sites in Sri Lanka. A 5th-century royal citadel perched atop a 200-metre volcanic rock, adorned with remarkable frescoes and mirror-wall inscriptions.

Enquire Now

Polonnaruwa

The Medieval Capital

Sri Lanka's second ancient capital and a UNESCO World Heritage city. Majestic ruins of palaces, temples, and colossal Buddha statues at Gal Vihara, set against vast man-made lakes.

Enquire Now

Ella

Mountain Village Paradise

A breathtaking mountain village perched at 1,000 metres with sweeping valley views. Hike to Little Adam's Peak, walk the Nine Arches Bridge, and arrive in style on the iconic Kandy–Ella train.

Enquire Now

Nuwara Eliya

Little England of Sri Lanka

At 1,868 metres above sea level, Sri Lanka's coolest city. Rolling emerald tea estates, colonial bungalows, rose gardens, trout fishing, and a colonial-era golf course wrapped in perpetual mist.
